Composer's Sketchpad

It's hard to experiment with music if you're not a virtuoso. A lot of remembering is involved. Pick out a nice melody? Now you have to keep it in mind going forward. Find a nice harmonic progression? Make sure you don't let it slip. And what about putting everything together?

Composer's Sketchpad makes composing music quick and intuitive.

Features

Flexible Notes

Don't feel constrained by antiquated notions of time and pitch: start your notes at any point and bend them to any pitch! Notate guitar solos and complex polyrhythms as easily as any choral.

Intuitive Drawing

Making music with Composer's Sketchpad is easy. Scroll around with your finger. Draw by using a second finger to the right of the first. Zoom by using second finger to the left of the first. Play back your music immediately. Rewind. Record.

Pencil Support

If you're a lucky owner of an iPad Pro, the Pencil is fully supported! Draw smooth, lugubrious notes with the swype of the sexy stylus.

Over 100 Instruments

Choose from a variety of instruments across many different genres, including keyboards, guitars, orchestral instruments, and many more. You can also use more than 50 different percussion notes.

Questions

  • What's with the weird controls?

    Most drawing apps let you draw with one finger and pan around with two. Personally, I've never had this work well; you often have to try multiple times to get it to work, and you can forget about momentum scrolling. For this app, I wanted scrolling to be seamless without compromising on drawing. The mental model is actually quite simple: you have to hold down the canvas with one finger before you start drawing.

  • Why no MIDI in/out support?

    One of the starring features of Composer's Sketchpad is the ability for any note to start at, and bend to, any pitch. Unfortunately, MIDI pitch bends are done per-channel, which means that the MIDI output for this app will be very complicated and ill-suited to most apps. If you know of a standard way to pitch-bend individual MIDI notes, I'd love to hear about it!

  • What features are in the pipeline?

    In terms of major features, MP3 export and iPhone support are the highest priority. I'll also be looking into Audiobus support. Of course, there are tons of smaller and more esoteric features that I'll be adding, too! (But I don't want to spill all the beans quite yet.)
